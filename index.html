About Me

I am a dedicated researcher with a passion for GIS and Remote Sensing. My academic journey has been driven by curiosity and the desire to contribute meaningful insights to the scientific community.

Currently pursuing my Undergraduate in Urban And Rural Planning at Khulna University, My thesis is on Urban Expansion with particular emphasis on GIS. My interdisciplinary approach combines a variety of methods to address complex challenges in the field.

Beyond research, I am committed to academic excellence, peer collaboration, and knowledge dissemination through publications and conference presentations.

Research Projects

Landuse/Land Cover Classification

Focused on satellite-based land cover classification using advanced machine learning and remote sensing techniques. This project involves analyzing multi-temporal and multi-spectral datasets to accurately map and monitor land use changes, supporting sustainable planning and environmental monitoring.

Remote Sensing Machine Learning Geospatial Analysis LULC

Susceptibility Mapping

Conducted hazard susceptibility mapping for cyclones, landslides, and droughts using geospatial analysis and machine learning techniques. This research supports disaster risk reduction and climate-resilient planning by identifying vulnerable zones and generating predictive spatial models based on environmental, topographic, and climatic variables.

Disaster Risk Cyclone Landslide Drought GIS Remote Sensing Machine Learning

Site Suitability Analysis

Performed spatial suitability analysis to identify optimal locations for various applications such as urban expansion, agricultural development, and renewable energy projects. This work integrates GIS-based multi-criteria decision analysis (MCDA), remote sensing data, and stakeholder-driven criteria to support evidence-based planning and sustainable land use.

GIS MCDA Remote Sensing Sustainability Land Use Planning
